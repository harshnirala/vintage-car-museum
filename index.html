This Month's Legend

Mercedes-Benz 300SL Gullwing

The Story

Born from racing heritage, the 300SL Gullwing coupe was a direct descendant of the W194 race car. The rigid tubular spaceframe chassis ran high along the sides of the cabin, making standard doors structurally impossible. The engineers solved this with upward-hinged roof-mounted doors, creating the iconic silhouette that shocked the design world.

Historical Significance

The 300SL revolutionized performance by introducing the world's first production gasoline direct-injection system, nearly doubling its horsepower. Capable of a blistering 163 mph, it was the fastest production car of its era, redefining sports car performance and laying the foundation for modern supercars.

Restoration Lab

Experience the magic of preservation. Slide back and forth to see the meticulous body and engine overhaul of a 1954 Mercedes-Benz 300SL Gullwing.

Barn Find
Restored

1954 Mercedes-Benz 300SL

This specific chassis #198.040 was discovered in an abandoned warehouse in 2021, untouched for 45 years. Our restoration team spent 3,200 hours completely rebuilding it to concours standards.

1

Disassembly & Cataloging

Every single bolt, spacer, and panel was removed, labeled, and logged in our system.

2

Chassis & Paint Restoration

Rust remediation on the spaceframe chassis followed by hand-sanded primer and 6 coats of original Mercedes DB-180 Silver paint.

3

Engine Mechanical Overhaul

The matching-numbers 3.0L engine was stripped down and rebuilt, retaining 92% of original components.
